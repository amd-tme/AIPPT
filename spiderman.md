# Spider-Man: The Complete Story

## The Origin Story
- Peter Parker is a shy, nerdy high school student from Queens, New York
- Bitten by a radioactive spider during a school field trip to Oscorp
- Gains superhuman strength, speed, wall-crawling, and a "spider-sense"
- Tragically fails to stop a burglar who later kills his Uncle Ben
- Learns the defining lesson: **"With great power comes great responsibility"**

## Powers and Abilities
- Superhuman strength — can lift up to 10 tons
- Wall-crawling via microscopic hairs on hands and feet
- Spider-sense: a precognitive danger awareness ability
- Expert acrobat and hand-to-hand combatant
- Genius-level intellect — builds his own web-shooters from scratch
- Web-slinging allows rapid travel across New York City

## The Suit
- Original red-and-blue costume sewn by Peter himself
- Web-shooter devices strapped to his wrists — his own invention
- Iconic spider emblem on the chest and back
- Later upgrades include the Iron Spider armor (Tony Stark)
- The black symbiote suit — powerful but corrupts its wearer
- Stealth suit, Spider-Armor, Future Foundation suit — many iterations

## Peter Parker's Double Life
- Works as a freelance photographer for the Daily Bugle
- J. Jonah Jameson publishes anti-Spider-Man headlines constantly
- Balances crime-fighting with school, jobs, and family responsibilities
- Lives with Aunt May in Forest Hills, Queens
- Romantic relationships with Gwen Stacy and Mary Jane Watson
- Secret identity is his greatest burden and most important protection

## The Rogues Gallery
- **Green Goblin** — Norman Osborn, Spider-Man's greatest nemesis
- **Doctor Octopus** — Otto Octavius, arms fused to his body after an accident
- **Venom** — Eddie Brock bonded with the alien symbiote
- **Sandman** — Flint Marko, transformed into living sand
- **Electro** — Max Dillon, a living electrical conductor
- **The Vulture** — Adrian Toomes, a winged criminal mastermind
- **Mysterio** — Quentin Beck, master of illusion and special effects

## Iconic Story Arcs
- **"The Night Gwen Stacy Died"** (1973) — one of comics' most tragic moments
- **"Kraven's Last Hunt"** (1987) — a dark psychological masterpiece
- **"The Clone Saga"** (1994–1996) — controversial but culturally significant
- **"Spider-Man: Blue"** (2002) — a love letter to the Silver Age
- **"One More Day"** (2007) — Peter makes a deal with Mephisto to save Aunt May
- **"Superior Spider-Man"** (2013) — Doc Ock takes over Peter's body

## Spider-Man in Film
- **Sam Raimi trilogy** (2002–2007) — Tobey Maguire defines a generation
- **The Amazing Spider-Man** (2012–2014) — Andrew Garfield's grittier take
- **MCU Spider-Man** (2016–present) — Tom Holland as the youngest Peter Parker
- **Spider-Man: No Way Home** (2021) — all three actors unite on screen
- **Into the Spider-Verse** (2018) — animated masterpiece, introduces Miles Morales
- **Across the Spider-Verse** (2023) — raises the bar for animated storytelling

## The Legacy of Spider-Man
- One of Marvel's best-selling and most recognizable characters worldwide
- Symbol of the everyday hero — relatable struggles beyond the superpowers
- Miles Morales, Gwen Stacy (Spider-Gwen), and others carry the mantle
- "Anyone can wear the mask" — the core message of the Spider-Verse films
- Decades of comics, animated series, video games, and blockbuster films
- Remains Marvel's most beloved and enduring superhero
